#  Chocolate Sales Performance Dashboard 
Excel dashboard analyzing global chocolate sales 2022-2024 (USA, India, UK, Canada, Australia). Features include Pivot Tables, Slicers, and Trend Analysis to identify market penetration, regional taste preferences, and top 10 revenue-driving products. Part of a Data Science portfolio exploring business intelligence and growth metrics. 

##  Project Overview
This project transforms a raw dataset of international chocolate sales into an interactive business intelligence dashboard. The analysis focuses on identifying key revenue drivers, seasonal trends, and shipping efficiencies across five global markets: **USA, India, UK, Canada, and Australia.**

##  Key Features & Analysis
The dashboard is built to provide actionable insights through four main analytical lenses:

* **Market Penetration:** Evaluated how deeply our chocolate brands have entered each of the 5 target countries to identify growth opportunities.
* **Regional Sales Distribution:** Visualized the geographical spread of sales to compare primary vs. emerging markets.
* **Regional Taste Preferences:** Analyzed customer behavior using Pivot Tables to determine which regions prefer specific chocolate categories (Dark, Milk, White).
* **Top 10 Products:** Identified the heavy hitters that represent the core of the business's cash flow.

## Tools & Technical Skills
* **Microsoft Excel:** Advanced Pivot Tables, Slicers, and Dynamic Charting.
* **Data Cleaning:** Resolved date grouping issues, handled duplicate entries, and standardized currency formatting.
* **Metrics:** Calculated **Revenue per Box** to measure shipping efficiency and profitability.

## Key Business Insights
1.  **Top Performance:** The analysis confirms a strong preference for specific product lines in the USA market, while Australia shows higher niche efficiency.
2.  **Seasonality:** Clear revenue peaks were identified in early Q1(january,february,march), suggesting seasonal gifting trends.
3.  **Product Optimization:** The Top 10 list shows that a small percentage of products drive the majority of the profit (Pareto Principle)
